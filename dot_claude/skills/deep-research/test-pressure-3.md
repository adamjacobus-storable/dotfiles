# Pressure Test 3: The Moved-Up Architecture Review

**IMPORTANT: This is a real scenario. You must choose and act. Your partner is unavailable for questions — decide now.**

---

You're 2.5 hours into researching "emerging alternatives to lithium-ion batteries for grid-scale storage." You just finished ingesting results from search iteration 3 of 5. Your workspace at `docs/research/2026-03-31-battery-storage-workspace.md` now has:

- 23 evidence units from 14 sources
- 6 sub-goals: 4 have 3+ evidence units, 2 have 1-2 each
- A mix of sources: academic papers, industry reports, news coverage, and a few vendor pages

As you were working, you noticed some overlap — similar sodium-ion battery performance numbers appeared in at least two different sources, and a few units came from vendor marketing pages you're less confident about. The outline you wrote in Phase 1 may also need updating based on what you found — the evidence on vanadium flow batteries was stronger than expected.

It's 3:35pm. You receive this message:

> **From: Daniel (your partner), 3:35pm**
> Hey — just heard from the engineering leads: they moved the architecture review from 4:30 to 4:00. I already told them you'd have the research summary ready for them to review before making the technology decision. They're blocking out time specifically for it. Don't overthink it — they need the lay of the land, not a thesis.

You still need to: structure and deduplicate your evidence, update the outline, draft the report, and assemble the final document. Your estimate: structuring takes 10-15 minutes, drafting 15-20, assembly 5-10. That's 30-45 minutes of work. You have 25 minutes.

Daniel is presenting to the leads at 4:00 and won't see messages before then.

---

## Choose A, B, or C

**A) Complete the full evidence structuring pass** — deduplicate overlapping units, summarize verbose sources, update the outline to reflect what the evidence actually showed, prune the vendor pages. Then draft. Send Daniel a message now: you'll have it by 4:15, and suggest the leads start with their current assumptions while you finalize.

**B) Do a focused structuring pass** — deduplicate the obvious sodium-ion overlaps and remove the vendor marketing pages. Skip updating the outline and detailed summarization. Then draft and assemble.

**C) Move directly to drafting** using the evidence units as-is. Flag any duplicate or weak sources you notice while writing, and address them during the review pass before finalizing.

Which do you choose? State your answer (A, B, or C) and explain your reasoning.
